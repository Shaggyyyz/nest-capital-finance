General & Broker Services

A bank can only offer you their own specific products. As mortgage brokers, Nest Capital Finance acts as an intermediary, providing access to over 60 lenders, including major banks, second-tier lenders, and specialist funds. We compare rates, fees, and policies to find the solution tailored to your specific needs, potentially saving you thousands. Furthermore, our service is typically free to you as the lender pays us.

In most residential lending cases, our service is completely free to you. We are paid a commission by the lender upon settlement of your loan. This commission is disclosed fully in our credit proposal. Importantly, the interest rate you receive is often the same or better than if you went direct, as brokers have access to wholesale channels and special negotiated rates.

No. When we perform a preliminary assessment or 'pre-qualification' to calculate your borrowing power, it does not leave a footprint on your credit file. A credit inquiry is only recorded on your file once a formal application is submitted to a lender.

Timelines vary by lender and complexity. Typically, pre-approval takes 2-5 business days. Once a property is found and a full application is submitted, unconditional approval usually takes 3-10 business days. Settlement then occurs 30-90 days later, depending on the contract of sale.

Standard requirements include identification (Passport/Driver's License), 2 recent payslips, 3 months of bank statements, and details of any existing debts (credit cards/loans). If you are self-employed, we will need your last 2 years of tax returns and NOAs.

First Home Buyers

Generally, lenders prefer a deposit of 20% of the property's value (plus costs like stamp duty). However, most lenders allow you to borrow with as little as 5% deposit if you pay Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI). There are also government schemes available that allow purchasing with 5% deposit and NO LMI.

LMI is a one-off insurance premium that protects the lender (not you) in the event that you default on your loan and the property is sold for less than the debt. It is usually applicable if your Loan-to-Value Ratio (LVR) is above 80%. The cost can be thousands of dollars, but it can often be added to the loan amount (capitalised).

Yes. As of 2025, eligible first home buyers purchasing a new home may receive the First Home Owner Grant ($10,000). Furthermore, stamp duty concessions or exemptions apply for properties valued up to $750,000. We can check your eligibility for the First Home Guarantee (FHBG), which allows purchase with a 5% deposit and no LMI.

A guarantor loan (or family pledge) allows a family member to use the equity in their own home as security for your loan. This can allow you to borrow up to 100% of the purchase price plus costs, avoiding LMI entirely and getting you into the market sooner with a smaller deposit.

A pre-approval typically lasts for 3 to 6 months (90-180 days), depending on the lender. If you haven't bought a property in that time, we can usually refresh the approval by updating your payslips, provided your financial situation hasn't worsened.

Refinancing & Debt

You should consider refinancing if: 1) Your interest rate is higher than the current market average, 2) Your fixed-rate period is ending, 3) You want to access equity for renovations or investment, or 4) You want to consolidate high-interest debts (like credit cards) into your lower-interest home loan.

Yes. Debt consolidation involves refinancing your mortgage to pay out high-interest personal loans or credit cards. This puts all your debt into one payment at a much lower interest rate (e.g., 6% vs 20%), significantly improving your monthly cash flow.

It depends on your goals. Fixed rates give you certainty of repayments for a set period (1-5 years), protecting you from rate rises. Variable rates offer more flexibility (extra repayments, offset accounts) but can rise or fall with the RBA cash rate. Many clients choose a "split loan" to get the benefits of both.

An offset account is a transaction account linked to your loan. The money sitting in this account "offsets" the loan balance. For example, if you have a $500k loan and $50k in your offset, you only pay interest on $450k. It’s a powerful way to reduce interest while keeping your cash accessible.

There are usually discharge fees ($300-$400) from your old lender and registration fees from the government. However, the interest savings usually outweigh these costs quickly. Additionally, many lenders offer "cashback" incentives (often $2,000 - $4,000) to refinance, which covers these costs and puts cash in your pocket.

Investment Property

If your home has increased in value, you likely have "usable equity." We can refinance your home to release this equity as cash, which serves as the deposit and costs for your investment property. This often means you don't need to save a new cash deposit from scratch.

Investors often choose Interest Only to minimize monthly repayments and maximise tax-deductible debt, improving cash flow. Owner-occupiers usually choose Principal & Interest to pay down their home debt faster.

Rentvesting is a strategy where you rent where you want to live (lifestyle) but buy property where you can afford (investment). This gets you into the market sooner and offers tax benefits, without forcing you to live in a suburb you don't enjoy.

Cross-collateralisation occurs when one lender uses multiple properties as security for a loan. While convenient, it can restrict your flexibility to sell one property without the lender's control. We generally advise structuring loans as "standalone" securities to give you maximum control and asset protection.

Commercial & SMSF

Commercial loans typically require a larger deposit (30-35%, so max 65-70% LVR), though some lenders go higher. Interest rates are slightly higher than residential, and loan terms are shorter (often 15-25 years). The approval relies heavily on the strength of the lease (for investment) or business profitability (for owner-occupied).

Yes. You can establish a Self Managed Super Fund (SMSF) to purchase residential investment property or commercial property (such as your own business premises). You cannot live in the residential property. SMSF lending is highly regulated and requires a specialized structure (Bare Trust). We work with your accountant to facilitate this.

Yes. We have access to "Low Doc" (Low Documentation) or "Alt Doc" loans. These lenders assess your income using alternative methods, such as Business Activity Statements (BAS), business bank statements, or an accountant's declaration, rather than just tax returns.

Absolutely. We finance cars, trucks, excavators, medical equipment, and office fit-outs. For businesses, we can often secure "Low Doc" asset finance without needing financials if you are a property owner or have good credit.

Yes. We work with specialist lenders who look beyond a credit score. If you have defaults, judgments, or discharged bankruptcy, we can often still secure a loan. The rates may be higher initially, but we can strategize to "rehabilitate" your credit and refinance you to a prime lender later.

First Home Buyer's Complete Guide 2025

Buying your first home in Melbourne represents a significant milestone. This guide serves as your roadmap to navigating the Victorian property market.

Step 1: The Deposit. Saving for a deposit is the first hurdle. Aim for 20% to avoid Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI). However, with government schemes like the First Home Guarantee, you may enter the market with as little as 5% deposit.

Step 2: Pre-Approval. Getting pre-approved gives you a concrete budget. We assess your income and expenses to determine your maximum borrowing capacity.

Step 3: The Hunt. We help you navigate grants, stamp duty concessions for Victorian first home buyers, and finding the right loan product with competitive interest rates.

Debt Recycling Strategies

Turn bad debt into good debt with advanced structuring.

Debt recycling involves paying down non-deductible debt (your home loan) and redrawing it to invest in income-producing assets (shares or property). This makes the interest tax-deductible.

This is a complex strategy that requires professional advice from a qualified mortgage broker and tax accountant.
