General & Broker Services

Investment & Refinancing

Consider refinancing if your rate is above market average, your fixed term is ending, you want to access equity for renovations, or you want to consolidate debt.

If your home value has risen, we can refinance to release "usable equity" as cash. This cash becomes the deposit for your investment property, often removing the need for actual cash savings.

Investors often choose Interest Only to lower repayments and maximize tax-deductible interest. Owner-occupiers usually choose Principal & Interest to pay off their home faster.

Rentvesting means renting where you want to live (lifestyle) but buying property where you can afford (investment). It gets you into the property market sooner.

Yes. Consolidating high-interest debts (like credit cards at 20%) into your home loan (at ~6%) can massively reduce your monthly outgoing payments.

This is where you increase your loan amount to receive cash in hand. This can be used for renovations, buying shares, a wedding, or a car purchase.

An offset account is a savings account linked to your loan. The balance is subtracted from your loan principal when interest is calculated, saving you money.

There are discharge and registration fees ($300-$500 approx). However, many lenders offer "cashback" incentives ($2k-$3k) to refinance, which often covers these costs.
