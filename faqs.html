Residential Lending

Ideally 20% to avoid Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI). However, you can purchase with as little as 5% deposit (plus costs) with some lenders. First home buyers may access schemes allowing 2-5% deposits. We'll discuss options including family guarantees that could eliminate the deposit requirement entirely.
Fixed rates lock in your repayment amount for 1-5 years, providing certainty but limiting flexibility. Variable rates can change with market conditions but typically offer features like offset accounts and unlimited extra repayments. Many borrowers choose a split loan combining both for balance.
An offset account is a transaction account linked to your home loan. The balance offsets your loan principal, so you only pay interest on the difference. For example, a $500,000 loan with $50,000 in offset means you pay interest on $450,000. This can save significant interest over the loan term.
Borrowing capacity depends on your income, existing debts, living expenses, and the property type. Generally, you can borrow 5-6 times your annual income. Use our calculator for an estimate, or contact us for a detailed assessment based on actual lender criteria and your specific circumstances.
Budget 5-7% of the purchase price for costs including: stamp duty (varies by state), legal/conveyancing fees ($1,500-$3,000), building and pest inspections ($500-$800), loan application fees, and LMI if applicable. First home buyers may be eligible for stamp duty concessions.
Yes, though requirements differ from PAYG employees. Most lenders want 2 years of tax returns and financial statements. Some offer low-doc options requiring only 12 months of business activity. We specialize in self-employed lending and can match you with lenders who understand business income.

First Home Buyers

Several options exist: First Home Owner Grant (FHOG) for new builds, First Home Guarantee allowing 5% deposit without LMI, stamp duty concessions or exemptions, and the First Home Super Saver Scheme. Eligibility varies by state and property type. We'll help identify all schemes you qualify for.
The government guarantees up to 15% of your home loan, allowing you to buy with just 5% deposit without paying LMI. Limited places are available each financial year. Income caps apply ($125,000 single, $200,000 couples). We can help you apply through participating lenders.
Yes, through the First Home Super Saver Scheme (FHSSS). You can make voluntary contributions (up to $15,000/year, $50,000 total) and withdraw them for your deposit. Benefits include tax savings on contributions. You must apply to the ATO before signing a contract.
As little as 2% with government schemes, or 5% with standard lenders (plus LMI). You'll also need funds for stamp duty, legal fees, and inspections. A guarantor loan could allow you to purchase with no deposit. We recommend saving as much as possible to reduce LMI and interest costs.
In Victoria, first home buyers pay no stamp duty on properties up to $600,000 and reduced duty up to $750,000. You must be 18+, an Australian citizen or permanent resident, and live in the property for 12 months. Similar concessions exist in other states with varying thresholds.
Both have advantages. New builds may qualify for the FHOG ($10,000 in Vic) and have lower maintenance initially. Established homes offer quicker settlement and established locations. Consider your budget, timeline, and lifestyle. We can arrange finance for either option.

Investment Property

Most lenders require 10-20% deposit for investment properties. With 20%, you avoid LMI. Some lenders accept 10% with LMI. Alternatively, you could use equity from your existing home as a deposit, potentially buying with no cash outlay.
Yes, this is a popular strategy. If your home has increased in value, you can access up to 80% of its value (minus your existing loan) as a deposit for an investment property. This lets you buy without saving a cash deposit and may have tax advantages.
Negative gearing occurs when your investment property expenses (interest, rates, maintenance) exceed rental income. The loss can be offset against your taxable income, reducing your tax bill. It's a legitimate strategy but requires careful cashflow management. Consult your accountant for personal advice.
Yes, typically 0.2-0.5% higher than owner-occupied rates. This is because investment loans are considered higher risk. However, the interest is usually tax-deductible, which can offset the higher rate. We compare rates across lenders to find competitive investment loan options.
Interest-only loans have lower repayments and maximize tax deductions, but you're not building equity. P&I loans cost more monthly but build equity faster. Many investors choose interest-only for 5 years then switch to P&I, or focus on paying down non-deductible home loan debt first.
Lenders typically count 70-80% of rental income in their calculations (accounting for vacancies and expenses). Existing investment properties can help or hinder borrowing - positively geared properties boost capacity while negatively geared ones may reduce it.

Refinancing

Consider refinancing if: your rate is 0.5%+ above current market rates, your fixed rate is ending, you want to access equity, you need better loan features, or your circumstances have improved. We recommend reviewing your loan annually. Even small rate reductions can save thousands.
Typical costs include: discharge fee from current lender ($150-$400), government fees ($200-$500), and new lender application fees (often waived). Many lenders offer cashback incentives ($2,000-$4,000) that can offset these costs. We'll calculate whether refinancing makes financial sense for you.
Yes, though options may be limited. If you have 10-20% equity, you may pay LMI again. However, some lenders offer LMI waivers for certain professions or loyal customers. If property values have risen, you might have more equity than you think. We'll assess your current position.
Break costs apply when exiting a fixed-rate loan early. They can be substantial - sometimes tens of thousands of dollars depending on the rate difference and remaining term. To avoid them, wait until your fixed period ends, or factor them into your refinancing decision.
Yes, debt consolidation is a common reason to refinance. Rolling credit cards, car loans, or personal loans into your mortgage can significantly reduce interest costs and simplify payments. However, extending short-term debt over 30 years can cost more long-term if not managed carefully.
Typically 2-4 weeks from application to settlement. The process involves application, valuation, approval, and settlement. We handle the paperwork and liaise with both lenders to ensure a smooth transition. Your repayments continue to your current lender until settlement.

Commercial Finance

We arrange finance for offices, retail shops, warehouses, industrial units, mixed-use properties, medical suites, and childcare centers. Each property type has different lender appetites and requirements. We match your property to the right commercial lender.
Typically 20-35% deposit for commercial property, depending on the property type, location, and your business financials. Some lenders offer up to 80% LVR for quality assets with strong tenants. Using residential property as security can sometimes achieve better terms.
You'll need: 2-3 years of business financials (tax returns, profit & loss, balance sheets), lease agreements for the property, personal financial statements, business plan for owner-occupied premises, and details of existing business/property assets.
Yes, commercial rates are typically 1-2% higher than residential rates due to higher risk. However, they're usually tax-deductible for businesses. Terms also differ - commercial loans often have shorter terms (15-25 years) and may require annual reviews.
Yes, you can purchase through a company, trust, or SMSF. Each structure has different tax implications, asset protection benefits, and lending criteria. We recommend discussing with your accountant to determine the best structure, then we'll arrange appropriate finance.

SMSF Lending

Yes, SMSFs can borrow to purchase residential or commercial property through a Limited Recourse Borrowing Arrangement (LRBA). The property must be held in a separate bare trust until the loan is repaid. Strict rules apply, so consult your SMSF accountant before proceeding.
Typically 20-30% for residential property and 30-35% for commercial property, plus costs. The deposit must come from the SMSF's existing funds. Lenders also require the SMSF to have sufficient liquidity after the purchase to meet ongoing expenses and member benefits.
Only business real property (commercial property used in a business) can be acquired from a related party. Residential property cannot be purchased from members or related parties. The property also cannot be lived in by members or their relatives.
Potential benefits include: rental income taxed at 15% (0% in pension phase), capital gains taxed at 10% (0% in pension phase), ability to lease commercial property to your own business, and asset protection. However, limited borrowing options and restrictions apply.
Most lenders require a minimum SMSF balance of $200,000-$250,000 before lending. After purchase, the fund should maintain adequate liquidity ($50,000+) for expenses and diversification. Your combined super balance and the property value influence maximum borrowing.

Asset Finance

Asset finance covers vehicles (cars, trucks, trailers), equipment (machinery, medical equipment, IT), boats, caravans, and business assets. Both new and used assets can be financed. We access competitive rates from specialist asset finance lenders.
With a chattel mortgage, you own the asset from day one (good for claiming GST upfront). A lease means the financier owns the asset until the end of the term. Novated leases are popular for salary packaging vehicles. Each has different tax treatment - consult your accountant.
Not always. Many lenders offer 100% finance for new vehicles and equipment. Used assets may require 10-20% deposit depending on age and type. A deposit or trade-in can reduce your repayments and may secure a better interest rate.
Yes, asset finance is often easier to obtain than home loans for self-employed borrowers. The asset itself provides security. Requirements vary but generally include ABN registered for 2+ years, GST registration, and clean credit history. Low-doc options available.
A balloon (residual) is a lump sum due at the end of your loan term. It reduces monthly repayments but means you'll owe money at term end. Good for cashflow management or if you plan to trade in. Consider whether the asset will be worth more than the balloon at term end.

Guarantor Loans

A guarantor loan allows a family member (usually parents) to use equity in their property as security for your loan. This can eliminate or reduce your deposit requirement and avoid LMI. The guarantor doesn't give you money - they provide additional security.
Most lenders accept parents, siblings, or sometimes grandparents as guarantors. They must own property with sufficient equity and demonstrate ability to meet the guarantee if needed. Some lenders have age limits. The guarantor should seek independent legal and financial advice.
If you default on repayments, the lender can pursue the guarantor's property to recover the debt. However, limited guarantees can cap exposure to a specific amount (e.g., 20% of property value). Guarantors should understand they're taking on real financial risk.
Yes, once you've built sufficient equity (usually 20% of property value through repayments and/or property growth), you can apply to release the guarantee. This typically takes 2-5 years. We'll monitor your situation and advise when release is possible.
Yes, with a family guarantee you can potentially borrow up to 105% of the property value, covering the purchase price plus costs. You'll still need to demonstrate ability to meet repayments. This is popular with first home buyers whose parents want to help without gifting cash.

LMI Waivers for Professionals

LMI (Lenders Mortgage Insurance) waivers allow certain professionals to borrow up to 90% (sometimes 95%) of a property's value without paying LMI. This can save $10,000-$40,000 on a typical loan. Qualifying professions typically include doctors, lawyers, accountants, and other high-income professionals.
Commonly eligible professions include: medical practitioners (doctors, dentists, specialists, veterinarians), legal professionals (lawyers, barristers), accountants (CA/CPA), engineers, IT professionals, architects, pharmacists, optometrists, and mining professionals. Each lender has different criteria.
LMI costs vary but typically range from 1-4% of the loan amount for high LVR loans. On a $800,000 loan at 90% LVR, LMI could be $15,000-$25,000. With a waiver, this is completely eliminated. The savings can be used for your deposit or other property costs.
Some lenders extend waivers to professionals in their final year of study or internship (especially medical interns and registrars). Others require full registration. We know which lenders offer the most flexible criteria for your profession and career stage.
Some lenders require minimum income thresholds ($150,000+) while others focus purely on profession. Maximum loan amounts also vary - some cap at $1.5M while others go higher. We'll match you with lenders whose criteria suit your income and borrowing needs.

Rural & Agricultural Property

Yes, though lender criteria vary significantly for rural properties. Key factors include property size, distance from town, water and power supply, and whether there's income-producing land. Standard residential lenders may accept properties up to 10-50 hectares; larger properties need specialist rural lenders.
Typically 20-40% deposit for agricultural properties. LMI is generally not available for rural land, so you'll need genuine savings. Some government programs assist first-generation farmers. Deposit requirements depend on property type, income source, and your farming experience.
Often yes, by 0.5-1.5% compared to standard residential rates. Rural properties are considered higher risk due to limited buyer pools and variable agricultural incomes. However, specialist rural lenders understand farming and may offer competitive rates for strong applications.
Lenders typically average farm income over 2-3 years to account for seasonal variations. They'll review tax returns, BAS statements, and farm management accounts. Diversified income (off-farm employment, multiple enterprises) strengthens applications. Drought and commodity price fluctuations are factored in.
Most mainstream lenders accept rural residential properties up to 10-20 hectares (25-50 acres) if the primary use is residential. Larger lifestyle blocks up to 50 hectares may be considered if close to town with no commercial farming. Beyond this, you'll need specialist rural or agribusiness lenders.
